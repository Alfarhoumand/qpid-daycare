Below is a **Qpid website design system** you can give to Claude, a designer, or a developer.

It is built around the direction we discussed: **Awwwards-level animation and parallax**, but softened for a **warm, trustworthy home daycare brand**. Awwwards has dedicated inspiration categories for parallax websites and GSAP-based animation, which fits the kind of interactive, scroll-driven experience you want for Qpid. ([Awwwards][1])

# Qpid Family Child Care — Website Design System

## 1. Brand feeling

**Qpid should feel like:**

A magical little home where children feel loved, safe, playful, seen, and nurtured.

The website should combine:

* **storybook warmth**
* **soft animation**
* **home daycare trust**
* **California/Oceanside freshness**
* **playful Awwwards-style interaction**
* **parent-friendly clarity**

The site should feel impressive, but never confusing. Parents should immediately think:

> “This place is beautiful, safe, loving, professional, and special.”

---

# 2. Brand personality

## Core words

**Warm. Playful. Magical. Safe. Clean. Gentle. Professional. Loving.**

## Design DNA

Qpid’s visual world should feel like a soft illustrated children’s storybook mixed with a modern parent-facing childcare website.

Use:

* rounded shapes
* floating illustrated objects
* soft cream backgrounds
* sunny colors
* gentle parallax
* childlike icons
* handwritten-style accents
* clean parent-readable typography
* calm sections for safety and enrollment

Avoid:

* dark backgrounds
* harsh neon colors
* aggressive motion
* corporate stock-photo feeling
* chaotic animations
* tiny text
* overly experimental navigation

---

# 3. Logo usage

## Logo feeling

The Qpid logo should be treated as the heart of the brand. It should appear soft, friendly, and playful.

## Logo placement

Use the logo in:

* top-left navigation

* footer

## Logo animation idea

On page load, the logo can gently appear with:

* soft fade-in

* floating heart or sparkle around it

Keep it under **1.5 seconds**. No long loading screen.

# 10. Motion system

This is where Qpid can feel “jaw dropping” while still being parent-friendly.

## Motion personality

Motion should feel:

* magical
* gentle
* floaty
* soft
* storybook-like
* never aggressive

## Main motion principles

Use motion to guide parents through the story.

Do not use motion just to show off.

The best Qpid animation style is:

> “A magical illustrated daycare world that gently comes alive as parents scroll.”

## Motion types

### 1. Hero mouse parallax

Use layered hero artwork.

Layers:

* back layer: sky, clouds, sun
* middle layer: house, tree, rainbow
* front layer: flowers, toys, hearts, stars
* text layer: headline and CTA

Movement:

* back layer: very slow
* middle layer: medium
* front layer: slightly faster
* text layer: mostly stable

### 2. Scroll storytelling

As the user scrolls, sections reveal like a storybook.

Examples:

* cards slide up softly
* icons pop in one by one
* background clouds drift
* timeline follows the scroll
* illustrations change from morning to afternoon

### 3. Button hover

Buttons can:

* bounce slightly
* grow by 2–4%
* show a tiny heart/sparkle
* shift shadow softly

### 4. Card hover

Cards can:

* lift 4–8px
* rotate 0.5–1 degree
* reveal tiny decorative icons

### 5. Page transition

Use a warm fade or soft wipe.

Do not use complex transitions that delay parents from finding information.

---

# 11. Motion accessibility

This is important.

Include a reduced-motion mode.

When a user has reduced motion enabled:

* turn off mouse parallax
* remove long scroll animations
* keep simple fades only
* stop floating background objects
* keep buttons functional without movement

This keeps the website beautiful and accessible.

# 16. Voice and copy style

## Brand voice

Qpid’s writing should sound:

* warm
* clear
* loving
* reassuring
* professional
* simple

## Copy rules

Use short sentences.

Avoid complicated childcare/legal language on the homepage.

Move detailed policy language into the handbook page.

Use “families” and “children” more than “clients” or “customers.”

## Example tone

Instead of:

> We provide childcare services for enrolled minors.

Say:

> We provide a safe, loving home where children can play, learn, rest, and grow.

Instead of:

> Contact provider for availability.

Say:

> We’d love to hear from you and share current openings.
